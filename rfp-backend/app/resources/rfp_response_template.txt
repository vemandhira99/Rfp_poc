1. Executive Summary
The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) has initiated the development and implementation of an Integrated Financial and Human Resource Management System (IFHRMS) to modernize and streamline its financial management and human resource processes. The proposed IFHRMS aims to bring together multiple financial and administrative processes under a unified digital platform, enabling improved operational efficiency, transparency, compliance with financial regulations, and enhanced decision-making capabilities across CBSE’s organizational units.
This Request for Proposal (RFP) outlines CBSE’s objective to design, develop, implement, operate, and maintain an integrated system that consolidates key financial and HR processes including budgeting, commitment management, sanction and expenditure management, accounting, asset management, project and grant management, employee lifecycle management, payroll administration, and related reporting functions. The RFP also emphasizes the need for a centralized platform that ensures role-based access, automated workflows, standardized master data management, integration with external systems such as payment aggregators and existing receipt systems, and robust auditability across all transactions.
In response to these requirements, we propose the implementation of a comprehensive IFHRMS solution built on a combination of ERPNext as the core enterprise resource planning platform and the Akashic Unified Data Platform for data governance, analytics, and rule-driven operational oversight. This combined approach allows the solution to address the operational transaction processing requirements described in the RFP while ensuring that governance, reporting, and institutional oversight capabilities are implemented consistently across all functional modules.
Within the proposed architecture, ERPNext will serve as the primary transactional system responsible for managing core financial and human resource processes including budgeting, accounting, payroll, asset management, employee lifecycle management, project tracking, grant administration, and receipt and payment processing. ERPNext provides configurable workflows, structured master data management capabilities, and modular functionality that aligns closely with the functional requirements described in the RFP for the IFHRMS system.
Complementing this core system, the Akashic Unified Data Platform will support data governance, analytics, and monitoring functions across the IFHRMS ecosystem. Through components such as rules-driven validation mechanisms, master data governance frameworks, and business intelligence capabilities, Akashic enables structured oversight of financial and administrative data while supporting consolidated reporting and institutional-level analysis required by CBSE.
The proposed solution has been structured to address all functional areas described in the RFP, including:
1.	Budget Management
2.	Commitment Management
3.	Contract Management
4.	Sanction and Expenditure Management
5.	Human Resource Management
6.	Receipt Management
7.	Payment Management
8.	Grant Management
9.	Project Management
10.	Asset Management
11.	General Ledger and Accounting
12.	Data Analytics and Reporting
13.	System Administration
14.	Master Data Management
In addition to supporting these modules, the solution architecture also accommodates the integration requirements specified in the RFP, including the use of a centralized payment aggregator for payment processing and reconciliation, interaction with multiple existing receipt collection systems currently in use within CBSE, and automated exchange of financial transaction information with banking systems for reconciliation and reporting purposes.
The implementation approach proposed by us emphasizes a structured project delivery methodology covering requirement validation, system design, configuration and development, testing, user acceptance, training, deployment, and post–go-live support. The project will be executed through clearly defined implementation milestones, with defined stabilization and operations phases to ensure system continuity and support after deployment.
The overall objective of the proposed solution is to provide CBSE with a unified IFHRMS platform that ensures consistency in financial and human resource processes, strengthens institutional oversight, improves operational transparency, and enables timely and accurate reporting across the organization.
By aligning the system architecture, functional coverage, and implementation methodology directly with the requirements defined in the RFP, DHIRA Software Labs aims to deliver a reliable, scalable, and compliant IFHRMS solution that supports CBSE’s operational and governance objectives.
2. Understanding of RFP Requirements
The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) has issued this Request for Proposal (RFP) to select a System Integrator for the design, development, implementation, operations, and maintenance of an Integrated Financial and Human Resource Management System (IFHRMS). The objective of the IFHRMS initiative is to establish a unified enterprise platform that integrates financial management and human resource administration processes within CBSE.
The RFP describes a system architecture that consolidates operational, financial, and administrative processes into a centralized digital platform capable of supporting standardized workflows, institutional governance, and reliable reporting. The proposed IFHRMS platform must support fourteen (14) functional modules and integrate with twelve (12) existing receipt collection systems, while ensuring system availability, operational support, and compliance with defined service level agreements.
The following sections summarize the functional and non-functional requirements identified in the RFP.
